Classic Sourdough Bread Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g bread flour
  • 350g water (room temperature)
  • 100g active sourdough starter
  • 10g salt

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour and water, let autolyse for 30 minutes
  2. Add starter and salt, mix until well combined
  3. Perform 4 sets of stretch and folds over 2 hours
  4. Bulk ferment for 4-6 hours at room temperature
  5. Shape and place in banneton
  6. Cold proof overnight (8-12 hours)
  7. Bake at 450°F (230°C) in a Dutch oven

Tips:

  • Ensure your starter is at peak activity
  • Maintain dough temperature around 76°F (24°C)
  • Look for 30-50% volume increase during bulk ferment

Rich Chocolate Cake Recipe

Prep Time: 30 mins
Cook Time: 45 mins
Total Time: 2 hrs

Ingredients:

For the Cake:
  • 250g all-purpose flour
  • 350g granulated sugar
  • 75g unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 250ml whole milk
  • 120ml vegetable oil
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 250ml hot coffee
For the Ganache:
  • 200g dark chocolate, chopped
  • 200ml heavy cream
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

Cake:
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line two 9-inch cake pans.
  2. Sift together flour, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat eggs, sugar, and oil until pale and fluffy.
  4. Alternately add dry ingredients and milk to the egg mixture.
  5. Stir in hot coffee until smooth.
  6. Divide batter between pans and bake for 30-35 minutes.
  7. Cool in pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto racks.
Ganache:
  1. Heat cream until just simmering.
  2. Pour over chopped chocolate and let stand 5 minutes.
  3. Stir until smooth and glossy.
  4. Let cool until spreadable.

Tips:

  • Room temperature ingredients will give best results
  • Don't overmix the batter after adding flour
  • The hot coffee enhances chocolate flavor but won't make the cake taste like coffee
  • Cake can be made ahead and frozen for up to 2 months

Storage:

Store covered at room temperature for up to 4 days, or refrigerated for up to a week.
